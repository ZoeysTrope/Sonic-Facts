    Sonic The Hedgehog (Jun. 23rd, 1991) (Sega Genesis)

    An error in the line of code prevents the "Press Start Button" text to appear on Sonic 1's title screen on the Genesis, which was fixed in later re-releases and remasters like Sonic Origins.

    Sonic The Hedgehog 2 (Nov. 21st, 1992) (Sega Genesis)

    Despite Sonic 2 being the first playable and mainline appearance of Tails, his first appearance is actually from the Master System/Game Gear release of Sonic 2, which came out prior to the main Genesis release.

    Sonic CD (Nov. 23rd, 1993) (Sega CD)

    Sonic CD is the first game within the series that gives Sonic a voice, however they're only for select few things, and got removed in the Sonic Origins remaster.

    Sonic The Hedgehog 3 (Feb. 2nd, 1994) (Sega Genesis)

    Despite Michael Jackson having involvement in developing songs in Sonic 3, mainly The Theme Of Knuckles, The Act 1 Boss Theme, and both Act themes to Carnival Night, Ice Cap, and Launch Base, he didn't like how they sounded with the Genesis soundchip and refused to have his name in the credits, only leaving Brad Buxer's name in the credits. Despite this, when Sonic Origins changed the songs for all of these things to avoid copyright, Brad Buxer's name is still in the credits despite him not contributing to the new music used in Sonic Origins.

    Sonic & Knuckles (Oct. 18th, 1994) (Sega Genesis)

    Sonic and Knuckles was originally meant to be a complete game within Sonic 3, however due to technical limitations, and a deadline for a partnership with McDonald's, Sega split the game into 2 games, allowing you to lock the two cartridges together to make Sonic The Hedgehog 3 & Knuckles. Proof of this being the case is inside an early prototype dumped online which shows Flying Battery Zone appearing as one of the first stages within Sonic 3, despite it's final placement being the 2nd Zone of Sonic & Knuckles, after Mushroom Hill Zone and before Sandopolis Zone.

    Sonic The Hedgehog 4 Episode 1 (Oct. 13th, 2010) (Mobile/PC/Wii/PS3/Xbox 360)

    The marketing campaign for Sonic 4 Episode 1 prided itself on how few characters were in it, due to the negative reception of the side characters at the time of the game's release.

    Sonic The Hedgehog 4 Episode 2 (May. 15th, 2012) (Mobile/PC/PS3/Xbox 360)

    Despite the followup game, Sonic The Hedgehog 4 Episode 3 being cancelled, in the files of Episode 2, there is a music track left over for an ingame teaser for Episode 3 that never appears in the game, but does appear in an early build and is easy to find online now.

    Sonic Mania (Aug. 15th 2017) (PS4/Xbox One/Switch/PC/Mobile)

    Sonic Mania is the only Sonic game not directly made by Sega, with them only overlooking and approving, as Mania was made by Christian Whitehead and Headcannon, two developers who also contributed in making the mobile ports of Sonic 1, 2, and CD, along with PagodaWest Games.

    Sonic Superstars (Oct. 17th 2023) (PS4/Xbox One/Switch/PC)

    Sonic Superstars is currently the only Sonic game using the classic formula to allow more than 2 players at one time within the main game, allowing up to 4 players at one time within the main story.
